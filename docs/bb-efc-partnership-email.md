TO: partnerships@essendonfc.com.au
SUBJECT: Bomber Boat — partnership opportunity (maiden voyage this Saturday)

Hi there,

I run Bomber Boat — a boat that sails Essendon fans to the footy on game day. Our maiden voyage is this Saturday, Round 8 against Brisbane at Marvel Stadium, and I wanted to reach out before we launch.

I think there is a genuine partnership opportunity here worth a quick conversation.

THE EXPERIENCE

Fans board at the Maribyrnong River and sail to the game. Return trip after the siren. Every passenger gets a welcome drink on board and we have a professional photographer on every trip.

Fares are $90 adult return / $40 under 15s. One-way fares are also available.

The boat currently holds 60 people and we still have seats available for this Saturday — we would love to have some Essendon Football Club faces on board for the maiden voyage.

THE SCALE

We operate in partnership with Yarra Cruises (yarracruises.com.au), and through that relationship we have access to the MV John Batman — a 200-person, four-deck vessel that already cruises the Maribyrnong River upstream to Essendon. For marquee games we step up to the John Batman and can put 200 members on the water in a single trip.

THE IDEA FOR THE ESSENDON FOOTBALL CLUB

Instead of the standard membership merchandise pack, give members a Bomber Boat return trip as a benefit — one sail to a home game per season, built into a membership tier. Something members genuinely get excited about and post about.

The commercial arrangement would be a flat annual fee rather than per head — clean, budgetable, and easy to promote to members as a genuine perk. We can talk numbers on a call.

SIMON MADDEN

I am fortunate to call Simon Madden a friend, and I think he would be an absolutely sensational host for one of the early voyages. One of the greatest Essendon footballers to have ever pulled on the red and black — 378 games, four premierships, a Norm Smith Medal — sailing fans up the Maribyrnong to the footy and sharing stories on the water. The content that would generate for the club, for Simon, and for media is enormous. It is the kind of moment that puts Bomber Boat on the map and gives the Essendon Football Club a story worth telling.

THE BIGGER PICTURE

The vision is a boat experience for every AFL club in the country. We are already building that out. Getting the Essendon Football Club deeply involved early — as a partner rather than just a passenger — is an opportunity to shape what this becomes.

I would love to put a couple of seats aside for you this Saturday if you want to see it firsthand. Otherwise, happy to jump on a call any time next week.
